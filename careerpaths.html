Team Development

Career Paths

Growing UX Talent

One of the most important responsibilities of UX leadership is developing clear, motivating career paths for designers and researchers. Having founded 6 UX teams, I've developed career frameworks that support both individual growth and organizational needs.

Dual-Track Growth

Effective UX career paths must offer two distinct tracks so that advancing in your career doesn't require becoming a manager:

Craft Track

For those who want to go deep on design and research craft.

  • Associate Designer / Researcher
  • Designer / Researcher
  • Senior Designer / Researcher
  • Staff Designer / Researcher
  • Principal Designer / Researcher

Leadership Track

For those who want to lead people and shape strategy.

  • Design Lead
  • Design Manager
  • Senior Design Manager
  • Director of Design
  • VP of Design

Competency Framework

Each level is defined by clear competencies rather than vague expectations:

  • Craft excellence — Quality of design work, research rigor, and technical skill
  • Scope & complexity — The size and ambiguity of problems you can own independently
  • Influence — Your ability to shape product direction and design culture
  • Collaboration — How you work with cross-functional partners
  • Mentorship — How you grow others around you

Why This Matters

Clear career paths drive retention, motivation, and performance. When designers know what growth looks like and have a path to get there, they do their best work. This framework has been instrumental in building and retaining high-performing UX teams.
