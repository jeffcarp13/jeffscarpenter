Process

Double Diamond

The Framework

The Double Diamond is a design process model that maps the divergent and convergent stages of design thinking. Originally developed by the British Design Council, it provides a clear, visual way to describe the design process and has become a cornerstone of how I approach product design and UX strategy.

Four Phases

01

Discover

Divergent exploration of the problem space. Research, interviews, observation, and data analysis to understand user needs, pain points, and the broader context.

02

Define

Convergent synthesis of research findings. Identify patterns, frame the core problem, and align stakeholders around a clear problem statement and design brief.

03

Develop

Divergent exploration of solutions. Ideation, prototyping, and iterative testing to explore a wide range of possible solutions to the defined problem.

04

Deliver

Convergent refinement and implementation. Select the strongest solution, refine it through testing, and deliver a polished product ready for users.

How I Apply It

Throughout my career leading UX teams across startups and Fortune 500 companies, the Double Diamond has served as both a practical framework and a communication tool. It helps me:

  • Align cross-functional teams — Give product, engineering, and business stakeholders a shared language for where we are in the process
  • Prevent solution-jumping — Ensure teams spend adequate time understanding the problem before rushing to solutions
  • Scale design operations — Provide a repeatable process that new team members can follow and adapt
  • Justify research investment — Show leadership why the discovery and define phases are essential to building the right thing

Adapted for Enterprise

In enterprise environments, I've adapted the Double Diamond to work within agile sprint cycles, integrating continuous discovery alongside delivery. This ensures that research insights flow directly into product decisions while maintaining the rigor of the full framework.
