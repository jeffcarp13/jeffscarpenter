Beyond the Screen

Sailing

Life on the Water

When I'm not leading design teams or building products, you'll find me on the water. Sailing has been a lifelong passion that offers the perfect counterbalance to the digital world — it demands presence, adaptability, and teamwork in the most visceral way.

British Virgin Islands

The BVI is a special place for sailing — crystal-clear waters, steady trade winds, and some of the best anchorages in the Caribbean. I organize annual bareboat sailing trips with family and friends to explore the islands, disconnect, and recharge.

What Sailing Teaches

There are surprising parallels between sailing and design leadership:

  • Read the conditions — Like user research, you have to understand the environment before making decisions
  • Adapt to change — Wind shifts, just like requirements — the best plans are flexible plans
  • Crew coordination — A boat runs on teamwork and clear communication, just like a product team
  • Respect the system — You can't fight the ocean any more than you can fight user behavior

Sailing reminds me that the best experiences — whether digital or physical — are the ones designed with respect for the people and forces involved.
