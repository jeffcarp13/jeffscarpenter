Case Study

Live Earth

Role

Co-Founder, CEO & Chief Product Officer

Domain

Real-Time Data Visualization & Mapping

Industries

Defense, Security, Enterprise Operations

Overview

Live Earth is a real-time data visualization and mapping platform designed to aggregate live data feeds from multiple systems onto a single interactive map, creating a visual operations center for decision makers.

The platform was purpose-built to decrease response times for key decision makers by providing unified situational awareness across complex operational environments.

The Challenge

Organizations managing complex operations — from security teams to emergency responders — rely on data from dozens of disparate IT systems, sensors, and real-time feeds. Decision makers needed a way to see all of this information in context, on a single unified view, to reduce response times and improve outcomes.

The Solution

Live Earth visualizes and animates millions of data points simultaneously from dozens of IT systems, real-time data feeds, and sensors. Key capabilities include:

  • Real-time aggregation — Bring live data from every connected system onto an immersive, interactive map
  • Time-series playback — DVR-like timeline controls let users rewind and replay data to reconstruct incidents instantly
  • Video integration — Live video from camera systems correlated with other real-time data feeds on the map
  • Intuitive interaction — Touch-screen navigation similar to smartphones, with familiar gesture controls
  • Scalable architecture — Handle millions of simultaneous data points without sacrificing performance

My Contribution

As Co-Founder, CEO, and Chief Product Officer, I was responsible for the product vision, UX strategy, and design execution. This included:

  • Defining the product vision and go-to-market strategy
  • Leading UX design and research for the platform's interface
  • Building the team from the ground up
  • Negotiating IP transfer from the U.S. Government
  • Raising $14M in capital investment
  • Transitioning prototypes from government R&D into a commercial product

Impact

Dozens IT systems integrated
