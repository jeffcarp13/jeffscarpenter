Case Study

Nucleus

Role

UX Design & Research Leadership

Domain

Enterprise Design System

Scope

Company-Wide Product Suite

Overview

Nucleus is an enterprise design system built to unify the user experience across a suite of web-based products. It serves as the foundation for consistent, accessible, and scalable UI across the organization.

The Challenge

As product suites grow, inconsistency creeps in - different teams build different patterns, components drift apart, and the user experience becomes fragmented. Development slows as teams reinvent solutions, and quality suffers without shared standards.

The Approach

Building Nucleus required a systematic approach to design operations:

  • Audit & inventory - Cataloged existing components, patterns, and inconsistencies across the product suite
  • Token architecture - Established a design token system for color, typography, spacing, and elevation
  • Component library - Built a shared library of accessible, flexible components
  • Documentation - Created usage guidelines, interaction patterns, and implementation specs
  • Governance - Established a contribution model and review process to keep the system healthy

Key Principles

Consistency

A unified visual language and interaction model across every product in the suite.

Accessibility

WCAG compliance built into every component from the start, not bolted on later.

Scalability

Designed to grow with the organization - new products adopt the system from day one.

Impact

Nucleus became one of 4 company-wide design systems I've developed across my career, each driving measurable improvements in consistency, development velocity, and user satisfaction metrics including NPS, CSAT, and digital adoption.
