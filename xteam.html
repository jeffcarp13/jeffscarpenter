Team Building

X-Team

The Experience Team Model

X-Team (Experience Team) is my approach to building and organizing cross-functional design and research teams that drive meaningful outcomes. The model emphasizes embedded collaboration, shared ownership, and continuous discovery alongside delivery.

The Problem with Traditional UX Teams

In many organizations, UX exists as a service function - a team that receives requests, produces deliverables, and hands them off. This creates bottlenecks, limits design influence, and disconnects designers from the outcomes of their work.

The X-Team Structure

The X-Team model solves this by embedding design and research within cross-functional product teams while maintaining a strong design practice:

  • Embedded designers - Each product team has dedicated design and research capacity, not shared resources
  • Design practice - A centralized design leadership team maintains standards, mentorship, and career growth
  • Shared rituals - Design reviews, research shareouts, and cross-team syncs keep the broader team connected
  • Continuous discovery - Research is ongoing, not a phase - teams maintain constant contact with users
  • Shared systems - A common design system ensures consistency even as teams work independently

Scaling the Model

Having founded 6 UX teams across my career, I've refined this model through each iteration. Key learnings include:

  • Start with embedded roles before building centralized functions
  • Invest in design operations early - tooling, templates, and processes scale faster than hiring
  • Build research capacity alongside design - research-informed decisions are better decisions
  • Create career paths that value both craft and leadership tracks
  • Measure team health alongside product metrics

Results

This model has consistently produced strong outcomes: building a 15-person team from scratch, advancing UX maturity across organizations, and driving measurable improvements in product quality, customer satisfaction, and team retention.
